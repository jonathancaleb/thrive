Project type: Protected Spring

Every water project needs repairs to keep the water.

Kigaya fishing village community

19 years later the water that Robert drinks everyday  tastes great now that it comes from a protected spring.
20 years ago, your generous donation enabled us to protect a spring for Kigaya Community in Uganda. The contributions of incredible monthly donors and others giving directly to Uganda mission now TUG allow our local teams to visit project sites throughout the year, strengthening relationships with communities and evaluating the protected spring over time. These consistent visits allow us to learn vital lessons and hear amazing stories.
The community members of Kigaya  no longer suffer from waterborne diseases like diarrhea and dysentery because they have clean water at their protected spring.
Children in the village used to suffer from skin diseases such as diarrhea because they to bathed in dirty water, problems like that and diarrheal disease have declined in the time since the spring was protected.
Protection of the spring is only one step along the journey toward sustainable access to clean water. Thrive Uganda Mission is committed to consistent monitoring of each water source. Our monitoring and evaluation program, made possible by donors like you, allows us to maintain our relationships with communities by visiting up to 2 times each year to ensure that the water points are safe and reliable. This is just one of the many ways that we monitor projects and communicate with you.
