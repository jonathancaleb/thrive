Areas of attention
Youth unemployment in Uganda.
Youth unemployment in Uganda stands at a level between 64% and 70%, the government is overwhelmed. But many youths in Uganda engage in jobs which they do not desire but they have no option while the unemployed resort to criminal activity.
Education  crisis in Uganda
In Uganda, 7 out of 10 children won't finish a primary-level education. Most adults haven't received an education, and therefore don't see educating their children as important - many prioritize child labor over education. 
Water and Sanitation crisis
7 million Ugandans lack access to safe water and 28 million do not have access to improved sanitation facilities. Furthermore, due to disparities in water access in Uganda, urban people living in poverty pay as much as 22 percent of their income to access water from water vendors. Spending such a high percentage of earnings on water reduces overall household income, limiting opportunities to build savings and break the cycle of poverty.
Healthcare situation in Uganda
The major challenges affecting the health system are the lack of resources to recruit, deploy, motivate and retain human resources for health, particularly in remote localities; Medical care equipment is still stoneage to the extent that the rich and powerful have to be flown out for treatment. Uneven distribution of medical facilities as well as privatization of the medical sector with 55% of hospitals being privately owned.
We believe  that we can reduce inequity if everyone can get access to clean water, good education and proper healthcare facilities.

Our mission is to provide relief through education,social service development, and improving lives of children in need, equipping them with skills to become self-sustaining and productive individuals.
What makes us unique is not defined solely by what we do, but by how and why we do it.

Thrive Uganda Mission is guided by our values.
Putting People First
We honor the dignity in all of us our shared humanity. We situate people at the center of our organization and our work. This requires a spirit of mutual respect, compassion and solidarity in all our interactions.
Optimism
We are impatient optimists by nature: we see the glass as half full and are motivated to confront problems that others consider impossible to solve.
Activism
We approach inequity with passion and courage; to us, it is more than a job it is a calling. We strive to be creative in our activism, both as individuals and collectively. We create intentional space to fuel our spirits in ways that strengthen resilience and connection.
Collaboration.
 We recognize that resources  are only a small part of what is needed to achieve our goals and that our impact is greater when we work with others.
Innovation.
 We believe that many of the most intractable problems can be solved only through creative and innovative solutions
we draw upon diverse sources for inspiration. We create freedom to experiment and take risks, in the interest of finding  better alternatives. We demonstrate a solutions orientation, and initiate action on ideas.
Inclusion. 
We believe that inclusion is an environment that actively welcomes, connects, and values all, while harming none.
This means we actively seek, involve, and listen to broad, diverse points of view. We initiate appropriate action when people are being marginalized or excluded. We continuously learn and adapt to multiple cultures and styles.
Shared expectations  and accountability
Everyone at TUG is responsible for making this a great place to work. Our work environment affects what we do, how we do it and what we are able to achieve with both our internal colleagues and external  organisation.

We are an approved charitable organization registered in Uganda. The company's books of accounts and Certification are available to the public at the NGO Board. The license  is not an endorsement by the state. Over 4 years of experience and accomplishment.

Thrive Uganda Mission was originally founded as Uganda Mission but the company was dissolved in accordance to the organizations by laws after the death of one of the original founders in 2012.
The original Uganda mission was originally focused on setting up churches and spreading christianity in Uganda.
The new board of directors realized that this was not sufficient to make a necessary impact on the people of Uganda.
Between 2014 and 2018 the organization undertook research to discover the  key problem areas in Uganda.
On April 12th 2019 the Board of directors voted on four key areas to focus on as well as continuing the work of Uganda mission.
The organization was revived in 2019  as Thrive Uganda Mission.
